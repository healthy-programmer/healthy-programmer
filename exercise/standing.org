* Standing as an alternative

Motivation:

When I turned 45, I started to have lower back issues.
If I wanted to sit correctly with my back straight, it required significant effort.
I soon realized that the human body is not designed to maintain a single position (such as sitting) for long periods.
The only comfortable positions are those that require no effort—lying down or sitting with a bent upper back, which is essentially
lying on the chair.

I felt sleepy and sluggish. I also realized that my body was inactive for most of the day: 8 hours of sleep, 8 hours of work, 1 hour
in the car—at least 17 hours of inactivity.

So I conducted an experiment: standing as an alternative. It required a little training, but after 4 months, I feel like
I don't want to sit on the chair again.

Benefits:

- shoulders are naturally handing / falling down in the correct position (not bent forward)
- elbows are free, it perfectly fit with my ergonomic keyboard
- my body is active: sitting was a resting position, but standing is active
- I do not get sleepy during work
- My caloric output is doubled
- My breathing is deep and natural: less depression and anxiety
- I enjoy sitting during lunch time
- When I get back home, I already feel warmed up and the rest of the day is active as well

Effort (Correct standing):

- knees are slightly bent, in order to avoid using joints as support
- toes are pointing forward not to the side
- this requires gluteus maximus and quadriceps activation (and this is the part where training was required for me)
- as a result, the pelvis is neutral (avoid anterior pelvic tilt that causes lower back pain as it is over-bent)

Extra:

- I am standing on absorption mat to have this springing feeling that relieves my legs, knees and feet
- From time to time I wave my hips like in dancing to keep my legs fresh
- I can stretch my calfs by standing on toes at the back edge of the absorption mat, trying to reach the floor with the heel.

Progress:

- I started with just a few hours a day at first
- But then I was able to extend the time to the entire working day (with a lunch break, of course, when I really enjoyed resting while sitting)
